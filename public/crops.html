Crop Reference

Malaysian crop guide

A quick reference for common Malaysian crops — seasonal windows, risk levels, and what to watch out for. Use this alongside the AI chat for better decisions.

🌶
Chili
Peak: Mar – Jul
High-demand but prone to market saturation. Prices crash when oversupply hits. Requires close monitoring of regional output.
High Risk3–4 monthsWater sensitive
🫚
Ginger
Peak: Jun – Oct
Strong export demand. Good hedge against chili price drops. Longer growing cycle but more stable pricing throughout the year.
Low Risk8–10 monthsExport crop
🥬
Kangkung
Year-round
Fast-growing with consistent local demand. Low investment, quick returns. Ideal as a gap crop between longer cycles.
Low Risk3–4 weeksLocal demand
🍅
Tomato
Peak: Nov – Feb
Moderate risk. Prices fluctuate seasonally and are sensitive to rainfall. Often oversupplied during festive seasons.
Med Risk2–3 monthsRain sensitive
🌴
Oil Palm
Year-round
Malaysia's dominant export crop. CPO prices are globally driven. High setup cost but long-term stable income once established.
Low Risk3–4 yearsExport crop
🍌
Banana
Peak: Apr – Aug
Fast growing with good local and export demand. Cavendish variety faces competition; local varieties have niche premium markets.
Low Risk9–12 monthsLocal + export
🍈
Durian
Peak: May – Aug
Premium pricing with massive China export demand. High investment but strong returns. Weather disruptions during flowering are a key risk.
Med Risk5–8 yearsExport premium
🌾
Paddy (Rice)
2 seasons/year
Heavily subsidised by the government. Stable but low margins. Good for food security planning. Mechanisation reduces labour cost.
Low Risk3–4 monthsSubsidised
🧄
Turmeric
Peak: Aug – Dec
Growing demand from health and food sectors. Low pest pressure. Good companion crop with oil palm smallholders.
Low Risk8–10 monthsHealth market
🥒
Cucumber
Year-round
Short cycle and reliable demand from wet markets. Prices dip during oversupply. Good rotation crop with chili or tomato.
Med Risk5–6 weeksWet market
🫐
Papaya
Year-round
Fast returns, consistent demand. Red flesh varieties command better prices. Susceptible to papaya ringspot virus — monitor closely.
Med Risk9–11 monthsDisease risk
🌽
Sweet Corn
Peak: Mar – Sep
Short cycle and good local demand. Popular in agro-tourism farms. Low input cost makes it a good rotation crop.
Low Risk2–3 monthsAgrotourism
